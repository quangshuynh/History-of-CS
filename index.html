Jacquard's Loom

Invented in 1804




Definition


The Jacquard's Loom, created in 1804 by Joseph Jacquard, was an advanced device that attached to power looms to tell the loom to form complex textiles. The significance of the Jacquard's Loom was that it allowed unskilled weavers to design complicated weaves on textiles. Furthermore, the use of human-machine interaction to write binary using punch cards would be later developed into the modern computer. Moreover, the loom can control the warp threads individually, which meant it could create very advanced patterns in the clothes with better accuracy and speed.






Function


The significance of the Jacquard's Loom had the basic concept of inserting punch cards to program the pattern of the weave. This was one of the first ideas that revolved around inputting data to automate a process. The holes in the punch card are read by the appartus and they tell the loom what pattern to create. Then, there are pins in the appartus that can read the punch cards. The holes tell the appartus which threads underneath should be raised for each pass. Eventually, the idea of punch cards made it to the first computers. Punch cards carried instructions for the calculations in the computer.
















Analytical Engine

Invented in 1834




The Analytical Engine as shown here.



The Analytical Engine was created in 1834 by Charles Babbage and Augusta Ada Bryon. It was not sucessful, but the idea was relevant. The analytical engine would store information on a memory unit that would allow it to make decisions and then carry instructions based on those decisions.

Calculator vs Computer




Calculators


Calculators can perform mathematical operations. They are small mechanical machines that helps the user solve large math operations easily. Although they are not as complex as computers, the first computer was a calculator called Pascaline






Computers


Computers make decisions. It is a device that helps to store, arrange, organize data and information as well as calculate it. A computer has a lot of programming language and more complex than a calculator. Computers can be used to email, edit videos, make video calls, and more.













The ENIAC

Invented in 1946

ENIAC stands for Electronic Numerical Integrator and Computer





1940s

The ENIAC was one of the worlds first computers, being made in the 1940s.


Details

The ENIAC was a large, stand alone machine. It used up a tremendous amount of electricity. It also had miles of wires and thousands of vacuum tubes. Compared to hand-operated calculaters, the ENIAC was considered extremely useful.


Other Cool Facts

  • The ENIAC was so big you could park a school bus inside it.
  • ENIAC took 70 hours to work out pi to 2,000 decimal places
  • The ENIAC was first designed to print firing tables for U.S. Army artillery, but the war ended before the machine was completed
